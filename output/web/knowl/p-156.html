The tensor product can also be applied to vector spaces. The tensor product of a vector that exists within a space \(V\) and a vector that exists within a space \(W\) would exist within the space \(V \otimes W\text{.}\) Thus, a system of \(n\) qubits exists within the space \(\mathcal{H}^{\otimes \mathcal{H}}\text{,}\) where the \(\otimes n\) superscript means taking the tensor product of \(\mathcal{H}\) with itself \(n\) times. Similarly, \(\ket{\psi}^{\otimes n}\) would represent the state \(\ket{\psi}\) tensored with itself \(n\) times.
