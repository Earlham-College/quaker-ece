The probability that \(\ket{\psi}\) will be in the state \(\ket{0}\) after measurement is 25% because \((\frac{1}{2})^2 = 1/4 = 0.25\text{.}\) The probability that \(\ket{\psi}\) will be in the state \(\ket{1}\) after measurement is 75% because \((\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 = 3/4 = 0.75\text{.}\)
