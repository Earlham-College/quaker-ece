1.
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}^{\dagger} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
Since this matrix equals its adjoint, it is Hermitian
2.
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix}^{\dagger} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
Since this matrix equals its adjoint, it is Hermitian
3.
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} -i & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{pmatrix}^{\dagger} = \begin{pmatrix} i & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
Since this matrix does not equal its adjoint, it is not Hermitian
4.
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1+i \\ 1-i & 1 \end{pmatrix}^{\dagger} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1+i \\ 1-i & 1 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
Since this matrix equals its adjoint, it is Hermitian
