This notation was first introduced by mathematician Paul Dirac and is known as "Dirac Notation" or "Bra-Ket Notation." At a glance, it can be seen that the vectors \(\ket{0}\) and \(\ket{1}\) are each normal and are orthogonal to each other. Additionally, any point in two dimensional space could be described with a linear combination of these two vectors, meaning they form a basis (we will discuss exactly which space they form a basis for in the following section). Put together, this means \(\ket{0}\) and \(\ket{1}\) form an orthonormal basis.
